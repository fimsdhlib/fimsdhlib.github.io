## Introduction

Released in 2007 by Dataverse Ltd., ArtSteps is a web-based platform for creating 3D exhibitions and spaces to display 2D files and 3D objects, such as paintings, photos, videos, and sculptures (ArtSteps, n.d.-a). Supported formats include JPG, JPEG, PNG, MP4, MP3, WAV, OGG, WEBM, OBJ with MTL and TIFF, and GLTF and GLB with embedded textures (ArtSteps, n.d.-c). ArtSteps’ functionality makes it ideal for museums, artists, collectors, researchers, and businesses looking to create an online space for exhibitions or to use as a virtual medium for storytelling. ArtSteps also has Google Play and Apple apps for viewing the spaces on a mobile device (note, new spaces cannot be created via the apps) (Google Play, n.d.; Apple App Store, n.d.). The current version in use is unknown, however it appears continuing improvements are being made to both the web-based platform, as the FAQs page refers to future additions to the tool (ArtSteps, n.d.-c), and the mobile apps, which were updated in October 2022.

While there are other platforms that offer similar services to ArtSteps, such as 3DExhibit (3DExhibit.art, n.d.) and Exhibbit (n.d.), these alternatives charge a fee to utilize their software. ArtSteps allows users to create an account and publish unlimited spaces for free, albeit with some restrictions (ArtSteps, n.d.-c). If a user wishes to publish for free, they are restricted to using the free 3D space templates, which do not allow content to be connected to other webpages, and they can only publish their spaces publicly. To publish a space privately, request a custom 3D design, or receive aid in curating a space or hosting a virtual event on the platform, the user must pay for ArtSteps’ professional services.

## Creating a Space

ArtSteps offers a user-friendly platform that walks the user through each of the steps in creating a 3D space. The user must first define the space by designing a custom space or selecting one of the pre-built spaces, then they can add the artifacts by selecting display cases and uploading their images, videos, 3D objects, and/or text files. Guide points can then be added to plan a guided tour through the space and the public information, such as the cover photo and title, can be set. When the editor is ready, they can preview the space/tour before publishing the final project. The 3D space can be embedded into websites or shared on social media using the embed code or link (ArtSteps, n.d.-c). ArtSteps also offers Help documentation (ArtSteps, n.d.-d) and a frequently asked questions (FAQs) page that provide useful tips to help guide the user in utilizing the platform and troubleshoot issues (ArtSteps, n.d.-c). Each of these factors makes for an easy learning curve for new users.

As easy as it is to use, ArtSteps is not without its flaws. For example, if the 3D space includes doors, they must be open for the guide points to function correctly (ArtSteps, n.d.-c). It is also recommended to use Google Chrome when working with and viewing ArtSteps spaces as other browsers, such as Safari and Mozilla Firefox, may present issues (ArtSteps, n.d.-c). Collaborative editing and backup/download options are not offered in the current version of ArtSteps, although they may be added in future versions (ArtSteps, n.d.-c). Finally, it should be noted that any work published publicly on ArtSteps could be downloaded by visitors; however, images uploaded to ArtSteps are subject to a default compression, which may aid in protecting the original version of the work.

## Viewing Spaces

Once a space has been published, it can be viewed via a computer, smartphone, or tablet. It can also be viewed using VR (Virtual Reality) goggles, which allow the viewer to become fully immersed within the 3D space (ArtSteps, 2020). The process of navigating the space will be intuitive for anyone who has used Google Street View in Google Maps and Google Earth. The user can zoom in and out using the scroll on their mouse and move around the room by clicking on the desired spot on the floor, which is marked by a pair of footsteps. An overall map view showing the user’s current location can be found in the lower left corner of the screen.

ArtSteps also provides an exhibition page which acts as a social media platform for publicly published spaces (ArtSteps, n.d.-b). Here, users can view the published spaces, communicate via chat, and leave comments. This social platform provides users with the opportunity to share their work with others and participate in a community of likeminded creators.

## Conclusion

Overall, ArtSteps is an easy-to-use platform that permits users to create exhibits and share their work in a 3D format for free. Software like ArtSteps is becoming ever more valuable in an increasingly digital world. These tools give creators the opportunity to share their work worldwide and enables them to preserve their collections in a way that physical spaces simply do not allow. ArtSteps was certainly useful in allowing creators to continue sharing their exhibitions during the COVID-19 pandemic lockdowns (for example, when UNHCR, the UN Refugee Agency, utilized the platform to hold an art contest during the pandemic) (ArtSteps, 2020) and it may continue to serve an important role in future if further lockdowns are required.
